# MIDNIGHT AT THE MAJESTIC: PHASE 2 - SYSTEMATIC CREATION

## SYSTEMATIC STORY STRUCTURE DEVELOPMENT

### **THREE-ACT PROGRESSION**

**ACT I: "OPENING NIGHT" (45 minutes)**
- **Inciting Incident**: Vivian Sterling's murder during intermission
- **Rising Action**: Cast becomes suspects, theater locked down
- **Stakes**: Everyone has secrets, everyone has motives
- **Act I Climax**: Detective Morrison takes control of investigation

**ACT II: "THE INVESTIGATION" (55 minutes)**
- **Midpoint Reversal**: Major secret revealed that changes everything
- **Crisis/Dark Moment**: Killer almost escapes, second murder attempt
- **Stakes Escalation**: Personal becomes deadly, past becomes present
- **Act II Climax**: Final confrontation in theatrical setting

**RESOLUTION: "CURTAIN CALL" (10 minutes)**
- **Climax**: Killer revealed through theatrical trap
- **Resolution**: Justice served, truth exposed, survivors changed

### **SCENE-BY-SCENE BREAKDOWN**

**ACT I:**
1. **"Places, Everyone"** (8 min) - Pre-show preparation, character establishment
2. **"The Show Must Go On"** (12 min) - Performance of "Midnight Serenade"
3. **"Intermission Secrets"** (10 min) - Backstage drama, tensions revealed
4. **"Murder in Dressing Room A"** (15 min) - Body discovered, investigation begins

**ACT II:**
1. **"Everyone's a Suspect"** (15 min) - Initial questioning, alibis established
2. **"Secrets in the Shadows"** (15 min) - Hidden passages, dark revelations
3. **"The Truth Takes the Stage"** (15 min) - Major revelation, stakes escalate
4. **"Final Performance"** (10 min) - Killer revealed, justice served

**2. "Queen of Broadway"** (Vivian Sterling, 5 minutes)
- **Function**: Establish victim's power and hidden darkness
- **Style**: Star vehicle, dramatic and commanding
- **Key**: E♭ major, regal and powerful
- **Structure**: Verse-Chorus-Verse-Chorus-Bridge-Final Chorus-Coda

- **Function**: Establish Ruby's desperation and ambition
- **Style**: Yearning ballad building to determination
- **Key**: F minor → A♭ major, darkness to hope
- **Structure**: Verse-Chorus-Verse-Chorus-Bridge-Final Chorus

- **Structure**: Individual verses building to complex ensemble finale

- **Function**: Show-within-show, interrupted by murder

- **Function**: Discovery of murder, transition to investigation
