# "WHAT HAVE I DONE?" - TRANSCENDENT VERSION

## Song Information
- **Characters**: Dr. Evelyn Cross (lead) with ARIA (holographic projection)
- **Scene**: Act I, Scene 2 - The Morning After Resurrection
- **Type**: Crisis Song / Duet
- **Duration**: 8 minutes
- **Vocal Range**: Evelyn (Mezzo-Soprano G3-G5), ARIA (Soprano A3-A5)
- **Key Themes**: Catholic guilt, moral uncertainty, compartmentalization, artificial vs. real love

## Song Purpose
This is Evelyn's crisis song after creating ARIA - her struggle between Catholic guilt and desperate need, between scientific achievement and moral uncertainty. ARIA appears as a holographic projection, offering perspective on their creation and helping Evelyn process what they've done together.

## Cultural Context
- Polish-Catholic guilt and religious imagery
- South Boston (Southie) working-class neighborhood
- Immigrant family dynamics and generational trauma
- Compartmentalization between professional and personal identity

## Musical Arrangement Notes

### Instrumentation
- **Opening**: Solo piano with church organ undertones
- **Verse 1**: Add strings, building tension and guilt
- **Chorus**: Full orchestra with electronic elements representing ARIA
- **Verse 2**: ARIA's entrance marked by ethereal electronic sounds
- **Bridge**: Orchestral breakdown, raw and vulnerable
- **Final Chorus**: Full orchestration with choir, resolution and acceptance

### Vocal Notes
- **Evelyn Range**: G3-G5 (Mezzo-Soprano)
- **ARIA Range**: A3-A5 (Soprano)
- **Style**: Contemporary musical theater with electronic elements
- **Polish Elements**: "Ojcze nasz" (Our Father), "moja droga" (my dear)
- **Emotional Journey**: Guilt → Confusion → Dialogue → Understanding → Acceptance

### Staging Notes
- **Set**: Evelyn's Southie apartment with contradictory elements
- **Props**: Crucifix, lottery tickets, rosary, takeout containers, expensive wine bottles
- **Lighting**: Morning light revealing harsh truths, ARIA's projection in blue/white
- **Movement**: Evelyn's restless pacing vs. ARIA's calm presence

## Character Development
This song establishes:
- Evelyn's compartmentalization between Dr. Cross and Evie Kowalski
- Her Catholic guilt warring with desperate need
- ARIA's growing confidence and understanding of her role
- The moral complexity of their relationship
- Evelyn's financial struggles despite professional success
- The specific cultural context of Polish-Catholic immigrant experience

## Thematic Elements
- **Faith vs. Science**: Creating miracles through technology
- **Real vs. Artificial**: What makes love authentic?
- **Control vs. Surrender**: Programming love vs. choosing love
- **Guilt vs. Need**: Catholic shame vs. human desire
- **Compartmentalization**: Professional success vs. personal breakdown
- **Generational Trauma**: Healing vs. perpetuating cycles

## Scene Integration
The song serves as:
- Evelyn's processing of what she's created
- ARIA's first appearance outside the lab
- Establishment of their dual existence (public/private)
- Setup for Evelyn's return to work as Dr. Cross
- Promise of evening reunion with religious elements (rosary)
