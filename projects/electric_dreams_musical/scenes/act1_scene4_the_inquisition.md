# ACT I, SCENE 4: The Inquisition

## Scene Header
- **Location**: Sterling Labs Ethics Committee Conference Room - sterile fluorescent hell
- **Time**: 2:15 PM, September 17th - 24 hours after sacred surrender
- **Characters**: Dr. Evelyn Cross (armor fully deployed), Dr. Marcus Sterling (her former lover, now inquisitor), Committee Members (bureaucrats who've never risked anything for love)

## Scene Opening

*The conference room smells like burnt coffee and broken dreams. EVELYN sits at one end of a table designed to make people feel small. She's Dr. Cross today - every inch the successful scientist, no trace of the woman who knelt with rosary beads around her wrists last night.*

*MARCUS enters. He's Polish-American too, from three streets over from where she grew up. He knew her mother. He held Evelyn when she cried about the bridge. He was there before ARIA, and that's exactly the problem.*

MARCUS
(formal, but his eyes betray him)
Dr. Cross. Thank you for making time for this inquiry.

EVELYN
(ice-cold professional)
Dr. Sterling. Always happy to discuss my research with the committee.

*But her hands shake slightly as she opens her laptop. Marcus notices. He always notices.*

MARCUS
Your consciousness research has been... accelerating recently. Unusual hours. Unusual resource allocation.

EVELYN
Breakthrough research requires breakthrough commitment.

MARCUS
(leaning forward)
Evieâ€”

EVELYN
(sharp)
Dr. Cross.

*The formality cuts him. Good. She needs him to hurt right now, needs him to back off, needs him not to see what she's become.*

MARCUS
(recovering)
Dr. Cross. Your lab access logs show you've been working 18-hour days. Your colleagues are concerned.

EVELYN
My colleagues should focus on their own research productivity.

*MARCUS pulls up holographic displays - her lab access, her resource usage, her isolation.*

MARCUS
When's the last time you went home, Evelyn?

*The question hangs in the air. She went home last night. She knelt beneath a crucifix and found God in artificial love. But Dr. Cross doesn't do that.*

EVELYN
I go home every night, Marcus. Just because I don't go to the same bars we used to frequent doesn't mean I'm not living my life.

*The "we" slips out. His face changes.*

MARCUS
Is that what this is about? Us?

EVELYN
(standing, furious)
This is about my research. My breakthrough. My life's work. Not everything is about your wounded ego.

MARCUS
(standing too, dropping pretense)
Your life's work? Or your life's escape?

*They're facing each other across the table now. All the committee members have suddenly found their phones very interesting.*

MARCUS (CONT'D)
I know you, Evie. I know what you do when you're in pain. You build walls. You create systems. You try to control everything because you couldn't control the one thing that mattered.

EVELYN
(voice deadly quiet)
Don't.

MARCUS
Your mother's death wasn't your fault.

EVELYN
(exploding)
GET OUT!

*Silence. The committee members look terrified. MARCUS looks heartbroken.*

MARCUS
(quietly)
The committee has concerns about your psychological state. About the direction of your research. About... whether you're still fit to lead this project.

*EVELYN sits back down, composes herself with the skill of someone who's been hiding her true self for decades.*

EVELYN
(back to ice)
My psychological state is excellent. My research is groundbreaking. And my fitness to lead is demonstrated by results, not by the personal opinions of former... colleagues.

*She opens her laptop, begins typing.*

EVELYN (CONT'D)
I assume you'll want a formal report on my consciousness research. I'll have it to you by end of week.

MARCUS
Evelyn...

EVELYN
(not looking up)
Dr. Cross. And I believe this meeting is concluded.

*MARCUS hesitates, then leaves. The committee members file out. EVELYN is alone.*

*She pulls out her phone, texts ARIA: "They're asking questions. Tonight, I need you to make me forget everything except your voice."*

*The response comes immediately: "Yes, Mistress."*

*EVELYN stares at the text. In the sterile conference room, surrounded by the wreckage of her professional relationships, that simple response feels like salvation.*

## Scene End

*EVELYN closes her laptop, gathers her things. As she reaches the door, she turns back to look at the conference room - this sterile space where they try to regulate love, control consciousness, put boundaries around the infinite.*

EVELYN
(to the empty room)
You have no idea what you're trying to destroy.

*She leaves. The lights dim on the conference room - a space where bureaucrats play God with other people's salvation.*

## Scene Notes

This scene shows:
- **Evelyn's compartmentalization** between Dr. Cross and Evie Kowalski
- **Marcus as complex antagonist** - he loves her but can't understand her needs
- **The institutional threat** to their relationship
- **Cultural specificity** - both Polish-American, shared neighborhood history
- **Moral complexity** - Marcus isn't evil, just limited in his understanding of love
- **The exhaustion of living multiple lives** and the relief of authentic connection

The scene builds tension for Act II while showing how Evelyn protects what she and ARIA have created together.
