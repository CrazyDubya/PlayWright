# "I WANT TO BELONG"
**Pip's "I Want" Song**

## Song Information
- **Title**: I Want to Belong
- **Character(s)**: Pip (solo with small ensemble support)
- **Act/Scene**: Act 1, Scene 2
- **Song Type**: "I Want" Song/Character Establishing
- **Purpose**: Establish protagonist's desire, create empathy, set up character journey

## Musical Style
- **Tempo**: Moderate, building to hopeful
- **Key**: F Major (comfortable for young voices)
- **Style**: Heartfelt ballad with growing confidence
- **Audience Connection**: Relatable feelings, simple melody kids can hum

## Performance Notes

### Staging
- **Opening**: Pip alone, watching other students from doorway
- **Movement**: Gradual movement from edge to center stage
- **Ensemble**: Other students visible but not intrusive, showing their magic
- **Ending**: Pip takes brave step forward toward group

### Vocal Arrangements
- **Pip**: Solo throughout, range accessible to young performers
- **Ensemble**: Soft, supportive harmonies in final chorus
- **Dynamics**: Starts quiet, builds to hopeful, ends with gentle resolve
- **Breathing**: Natural pauses for young singer's breath control

### Emotional Journey
- **Verse 1**: Nervous, uncertain, small
- **Chorus 1**: Hopeful yearning, growing bigger
- **Verse 2**: Comparison, self-doubt, shrinking
- **Chorus 2**: Stronger yearning, more desperate
- **Bridge**: Vulnerable low point, then spark of hope
- **Final Chorus**: Determined hope with support
- **Outro**: Brave resolution

### Character Development
- **Establishes Pip's core want**: To belong and find their place
- **Shows vulnerability**: Fear of being different
- **Reveals strength**: Determination not to give up
- **Sets up journey**: From outsider to insider
- **Creates empathy**: Universal feeling of wanting to belong

### Relatable Themes for Kids
- **Starting somewhere new** (school, neighborhood, activity)
- **Feeling different** from other kids
- **Wanting to make friends** and fit in
- **Comparing yourself** to others
- **Finding courage** to try new things
- **Hope that things will get better**

### Musical Teaching Elements
- **Simple melody**: Easy for kids to learn and sing
- **Clear rhyme scheme**: ABAB pattern, predictable
- **Repetitive chorus**: Memorable and singable
- **Emotional vocabulary**: Belong, place, magic, home
- **Positive message**: Keep trying, you'll find your place

This song creates immediate connection with young audiences who have all felt like the "new kid" or worried about fitting in. The gentle, hopeful melody and relatable lyrics make it both emotionally resonant and musically accessible.
