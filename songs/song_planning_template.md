## Song Types in Musicals

### Opening Number
- Establishes world and tone
- Introduces ensemble
- Sets up premise

### "I Want" Song
- Character's main desire/goal
- Usually protagonist in Act I
- Drives the plot forward

### "I Am" Song
- Character introduction
- Establishes personality
- Shows current situation

### Plot Song
- Advances story significantly
- Reveals information
- Creates turning points

### Comedy Song
- Provides comic relief
- Character-driven humor
- Lightens dramatic tension

### Love Song/Duet
- Romantic development
- Emotional connection
- Character relationships

### Villain Song
- Antagonist's motivation
- Creates menace/threat
- Often elaborate and theatrical

### Ensemble Number
- Group dynamics
- Community/society themes
- Spectacular staging

### 11 O'Clock Number
- Late Act II showstopper
- Character's big moment
- Emotional climax

### Finale
- Resolution and celebration
- Brings themes together
- Emotional catharsis

## Song Placement Strategy
- Balance solo and group numbers
- Alternate high and low energy
- Consider vocal rest for performers
- Plan for scene changes
- Build to emotional peaks
