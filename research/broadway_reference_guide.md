# Broadway Reference Guide

## Classic Musical Theater Structure

### Golden Age Musicals (1940s-1960s)
**Characteristics:**
- Book, music, and lyrics fully integrated
- Character-driven songs
- Clear narrative structure
- Optimistic themes
- Traditional orchestration

**Examples to Study:**
- *Oklahoma!* - Integration of song, dance, story
- *West Side Story* - Modern themes, complex characters
- *My Fair Lady* - Character transformation
- *The Sound of Music* - Family themes, memorable songs

### Contemporary Musicals (1970s-Present)
**Characteristics:**
- Concept musicals
- Rock/pop influences
- Complex themes
- Non-linear narratives
- Diverse musical styles

**Examples to Study:**
- *Hamilton* - Hip-hop integration, historical reimagining
- *Dear Evan Hansen* - Contemporary issues, social media
- *The Book of Mormon* - Satire, irreverent humor
- *Wicked* - Backstory/perspective shift

## Song Types and Functions

### Act I Songs
1. **Opening Number**
   - *"Comedy Tonight"* (A Funny Thing...)
   - *"Tradition"* (Fiddler on the Roof)
   - *"Alexander Hamilton"* (Hamilton)

2. **"I Want" Songs**
   - *"Somewhere Out There"* (An American Tail)
   - *"Part of Your World"* (The Little Mermaid)
   - *"Wishing You Were Somehow Here Again"* (Phantom)

3. **Character Introduction**
   - *"The Wizard and I"* (Wicked)
   - *"Good Morning Baltimore"* (Hairspray)
   - *"Wait for It"* (Hamilton)

### Act II Songs
1. **11 O'Clock Numbers**
   - *"Defying Gravity"* (Wicked)
   - *"Being Alive"* (Company)
   - *"Rose's Turn"* (Gypsy)

2. **Resolution Songs**
   - *"Finale"* (Les Misérables)
   - *"For Good"* (Wicked)
   - *"One Day More"* (Les Misérables)

## Character Archetypes

### Protagonist Types
- **The Dreamer**: Wants to escape/achieve something
- **The Innocent**: Learns about the world
- **The Rebel**: Fights against system/authority
- **The Lover**: Seeks romantic fulfillment
- **The Hero**: Saves others/community

### Supporting Character Functions
- **Mentor**: Guides protagonist
- **Threshold Guardian**: Tests protagonist
- **Shapeshifter**: Loyalty unclear
- **Shadow**: Represents protagonist's dark side
- **Trickster**: Provides comic relief

## Technical Considerations

### Vocal Requirements
- **Soprano**: High, light voice (Christine in Phantom)
- **Mezzo-Soprano**: Middle range (Elphaba in Wicked)
- **Alto**: Lower female voice (Rizzo in Grease)
- **Tenor**: High male voice (Marius in Les Mis)
- **Baritone**: Middle male voice (Beast in Beauty and the Beast)
- **Bass**: Low male voice (Javert in Les Mis)
