# Technical Requirements Template

## Production Technical Specifications

### Venue Requirements

#### Theater Specifications
- **Proscenium Width**: Minimum stage opening width
- **Stage Depth**: Required playing area depth
- **Fly Gallery**: Height needed for flying scenery
- **Wing Space**: Side stage area requirements
- **Orchestra Pit**: Size and configuration needed
- **Seating Capacity**: Target audience size range

#### Loading/Storage
- **Load-In Access**: Truck access and door sizes
- **Storage Space**: Scenery, costume, prop storage
- **Rehearsal Space**: Separate rehearsal room needs
- **Dressing Rooms**: Number and size requirements
- **Green Room**: Cast and crew gathering space

### Scenic Design Requirements

#### Set Pieces
- **Major Scenic Elements**: 
  - Permanent structures
  - Moving pieces
  - Platforms and levels
  - Backdrop requirements
- **Scene Changes**: 
  - Number of different settings
  - Change timing requirements
  - Automated vs. manual changes
  - Blackout vs. visible changes

#### Special Scenic Effects
- **Flying Effects**: Rigging requirements
- **Trap Doors**: Floor access needs
- **Revolving Elements**: Turntables or rotating pieces
- **Projection Surfaces**: Screens or projection areas
- **Water Effects**: Fountains, rain, etc.
- **Fire Effects**: Controlled flame or smoke

### Lighting Design Requirements

#### Basic Lighting
- **General Wash**: Full stage coverage
- **Area Lighting**: Specific zone control
- **Color Requirements**: Gel and LED color needs
- **Intensity Range**: Dimming capabilities needed
- **Control System**: Board complexity requirements

#### Special Lighting Effects
- **Follow Spots**: Number and positioning
- **Moving Lights**: Automated fixture needs
- **Strobes/Effects**: Special effect lighting
- **Haze/Fog**: Atmospheric effects
- **Gobos/Patterns**: Projected images or patterns
- **UV/Blacklight**: Special visibility effects

#### Cue Complexity
- **Number of Cues**: Total lighting changes
- **Timing Precision**: Critical timing moments
- **Manual vs. Automated**: Operator requirements
- **Backup Systems**: Redundancy needs
- **House Lights**: Audience area control

### Sound Design Requirements

#### Reinforcement System
- **Vocal Microphones**: 
  - Number of wireless body mics
  - Handheld microphones
  - Headset vs. lavalier preferences
  - Backup microphone needs
- **Instrumental Amplification**:
  - Electric instrument inputs
  - Acoustic instrument pickup
  - Piano amplification
  - Drum microphones

#### Playback System
- **Sound Effects**: Pre-recorded audio cues
- **Underscoring**: Backing tracks if needed
- **Entrance Music**: Character or scene music
- **Transition Music**: Between-scene audio
- **Emergency Tracks**: Backup recordings

#### Audio Processing
- **Mixing Console**: Channel and feature requirements
- **Effects Processing**: Reverb, delay, EQ needs
- **Recording Capability**: Archival or broadcast needs
- **Monitoring System**: Stage monitor requirements
- **Intercom System**: Crew communication needs

### Costume Requirements

#### Basic Costume Needs
- **Period/Style**: Historical accuracy requirements
- **Number of Costumes**: Total costume count
- **Principal Costumes**: Lead character requirements
- **Ensemble Costumes**: Chorus costume needs
- **Specialty Costumes**: Unique or character-specific pieces

#### Costume Changes
- **Quick Changes**: Rapid costume switches
- **Change Locations**: Backstage change areas
- **Dresser Requirements**: Assistance needed
- **Preset Costumes**: Pre-show preparation
- **Laundry/Maintenance**: Cleaning and repair needs

#### Special Costume Effects
- **Breakaway Costumes**: Designed to come apart
- **Reveal Costumes**: Hidden costume elements
- **Period Undergarments**: Historical accuracy
- **Specialty Footwear**: Dance shoes, character shoes
- **Accessories**: Hats, jewelry, props worn

### Orchestra/Music Requirements

#### Instrumentation
- **String Section**: Violin, viola, cello, bass requirements
- **Woodwind Section**: Flute, clarinet, saxophone needs
- **Brass Section**: Trumpet, trombone, horn requirements
- **Percussion**: Drum set, timpani, mallet instruments
- **Keyboard**: Piano, synthesizer, organ needs
- **Guitar/Bass**: Electric and acoustic requirements

#### Music Direction
- **Conductor Position**: Pit vs. stage placement
- **Music Stands/Lights**: Orchestra setup needs
- **Score Requirements**: Full orchestration vs. reduction
- **Rehearsal Piano**: Separate rehearsal instrument
- **Music Storage**: Score and part organization

#### Audio for Orchestra
- **Orchestra Microphones**: Pickup for reinforcement
- **Monitor System**: Musicians hearing vocals/each other
- **Click Track**: Tempo assistance if needed
- **Communication**: Conductor to sound operator
- **Recording**: Orchestra recording capabilities

### Special Effects Requirements

#### Pyrotechnics
- **Flash Pots**: Controlled explosions
- **Flame Effects**: Open flame requirements
- **Smoke Effects**: Fog, haze, smoke machines
- **Sparkle Effects**: Gerbs, fountains
- **Safety Protocols**: Fire marshal requirements

#### Mechanical Effects
- **Flying Rigs**: Actor flying systems
- **Automation**: Motorized scenery movement
- **Trap Systems**: Appearing/disappearing effects
- **Projection Mapping**: Advanced video projection
- **Holographic Effects**: 3D visual illusions

### Safety Requirements

#### Fire Safety
- **Sprinkler Systems**: Automatic fire suppression
- **Fire Exits**: Adequate egress for cast/crew
- **Fire Extinguishers**: Portable suppression equipment
- **Flame Retardant Materials**: Treated fabrics and sets
- **Emergency Lighting**: Battery backup systems

#### Electrical Safety
- **Ground Fault Protection**: GFCI requirements
- **Load Calculations**: Electrical capacity needs
- **Cable Management**: Safe routing of electrical cables
- **Emergency Shutoffs**: Quick power disconnection
- **Qualified Electricians**: Licensed personnel requirements

#### Structural Safety
- **Weight Limits**: Platform and rigging capacity
- **Railings/Guards**: Fall protection systems
- **Non-Slip Surfaces**: Safe walking/dancing areas
- **Emergency Procedures**: Evacuation and injury protocols
- **Insurance Requirements**: Liability coverage needs

### Staffing Requirements

#### Technical Crew
- **Stage Manager**: Production coordination
- **Assistant Stage Managers**: Backstage coordination
- **Light Board Operator**: Lighting control
- **Sound Board Operator**: Audio control
- **Fly Operator**: Rigging system operation
- **Deck Crew**: Scenery movement

#### Wardrobe Crew
- **Wardrobe Supervisor**: Costume maintenance
- **Dressers**: Quick change assistance
- **Laundry/Maintenance**: Costume care
- **Wig/Makeup**: Hair and makeup artists

#### Front of House
- **House Manager**: Audience services
- **Ushers**: Seating and assistance
- **Box Office**: Ticket sales and service
- **Security**: Venue and audience safety
- **Concessions**: Food and beverage service

### Budget Considerations

#### Capital Expenses
- **Set Construction**: Materials and labor
- **Costume Creation**: Fabric and construction
- **Equipment Rental**: Specialized technical gear
- **Instrument Rental**: Orchestra instruments
- **Software/Technology**: Control systems and software

#### Operating Expenses
- **Crew Wages**: Technical and support staff
- **Utilities**: Power, water, heating/cooling
- **Consumables**: Gels, batteries, makeup
- **Maintenance**: Equipment repair and upkeep
- **Insurance**: Production liability coverage

### Venue Adaptability

#### Touring Considerations
- **Portable Elements**: Easily transported components
- **Venue Variations**: Adaptability to different theaters
- **Setup Time**: Load-in and technical rehearsal needs
- **Local Crew**: Skills available in different markets
- **Transportation**: Truck and shipping requirements

#### Scale Variations
- **Full Production**: Complete technical realization
- **Reduced Production**: Simplified technical requirements
- **Concert Version**: Minimal staging needs
- **Educational Version**: School-appropriate requirements
- **Community Theater**: Amateur-friendly adaptations
