#### Principal Characters
- **Lead Voices**: Who has featured solo moments
- **Character Arcs**: How this serves individual journeys
- **Relationships**: How characters interact within the number
- **Conflicts**: Disagreements or tensions expressed

#### Ensemble Roles
- **Character Types**: (Townspeople, workers, students, etc.)

### Musical Structure

#### Vocal Arrangements
- **Solo Sections**: Featured individual moments
- **Duet/Small Group**: 2-4 voice combinations
- **Full Ensemble**: Everyone singing together
- **Call and Response**: Interactive vocal patterns
- **Harmony Parts**: Soprano, Alto, Tenor, Bass divisions

#### Lyrical Content
- **Verse Structure**: Individual or small group verses
- **Chorus**: Unifying group message
- **Bridge**: Contrasting perspective or information
- **Tag/Outro**: How the song concludes
- **Repeated Phrases**: Memorable hooks or motifs

### Staging Concepts

#### Spatial Organization
- **Formation Changes**: How group moves and reshapes
- **Focus Shifts**: Directing audience attention
- **Levels**: Using platforms, stairs, varying heights
- **Entrances/Exits**: How ensemble appears and disappears
- **Isolation**: Featuring individuals within the group

#### Movement Styles
- **Choreographic Approach**: Dance style and complexity
- **Character Movement**: How different types move
- **Unison Moments**: When everyone moves together
- **Counterpoint**: Contrasting movements simultaneously
- **Stillness**: Strategic moments of no movement

### Technical Requirements

#### Vocal Demands
- **Range Requirements**: Highest and lowest notes
- **Stamina**: Vocal endurance needed
- **Harmony Complexity**: Difficulty of parts
- **Solo Capabilities**: Featured singer requirements
- **Microphone Needs**: Individual vs. area mics

#### Costume Considerations
- **Base Costumes**: Standard ensemble look
- **Variations**: Individual character distinctions
- **Quick Changes**: If needed during number
- **Movement Requirements**: Flexibility for choreography
- **Period/Style**: Historical accuracy or concept

#### Set/Props
- **Scenic Elements**: Platforms, stairs, set pieces
- **Hand Props**: Items carried or used by ensemble
- **Furniture**: Tables, chairs, practical pieces
- **Special Effects**: Smoke, confetti, projections
- **Safety**: Ensuring safe movement with props/set

### Dramatic Moments

#### Emotional Peaks
- **Build-Up**: How energy increases
- **Climax**: Highest emotional moment
- **Release**: How tension resolves
- **Quiet Moments**: Intimate or reflective beats
- **Surprise Elements**: Unexpected turns or reveals

#### Conflict Integration
- **Opposing Factions**: Different groups with different goals
- **Debate/Argument**: Sung disagreements
- **Unity Moments**: When conflicts resolve
- **Tension Building**: Increasing dramatic pressure
- **Resolution**: How conflicts are addressed

### Audience Engagement

#### Participation Elements
- **Clap-Along**: Rhythmic audience involvement
- **Sing-Along**: Memorable, repeatable phrases
- **Call and Response**: Audience interaction
- **Visual Spectacle**: Impressive staging moments
- **Emotional Connection**: Relatable human moments

#### Entertainment Value
- **Comedy Elements**: Humorous moments or characters
- **Spectacle**: Visually impressive staging
- **Surprise**: Unexpected developments
- **Beauty**: Aesthetically pleasing moments
- **Energy**: High-impact, exciting sequences

### Production Considerations

#### Casting Requirements
- **Vocal Types**: Range of voice parts needed
- **Acting Ability**: Character work within ensemble
- **Dance Skills**: Movement requirements
- **Age Range**: Appropriate casting spread
- **Diversity**: Representation considerations

#### Rehearsal Needs
- **Music Rehearsal**: Learning vocal parts
- **Staging Rehearsal**: Blocking and choreography
- **Technical Rehearsal**: With full production elements
- **Ensemble Building**: Creating group cohesion
- **Individual Coaching**: Featured performer work

#### Budget Impact
- **Costume Costs**: Multiple ensemble costumes
- **Set Requirements**: Scenic elements needed
- **Orchestration**: Musical arrangement complexity
- **Rehearsal Time**: Extended preparation needed
- **Special Effects**: Additional technical costs

### Integration Points

#### Scene Transitions
- **Into the Number**: How it begins naturally
- **Out of the Number**: Smooth transition to dialogue/next scene
- **Underscoring**: Music continuing under dialogue
- **Segue**: Direct connection to next musical moment
- **Blackout**: Clean ending with scene change

#### Character Continuity
- **Before**: Character states entering the number
- **During**: How characters change within the song
- **After**: Character states leaving the number
- **Consistency**: Maintaining character truth throughout
- **Growth**: How the number serves character arcs
