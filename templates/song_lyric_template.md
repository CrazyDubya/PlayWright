## Lyrical Guidelines

### Rhyme Schemes
- **ABAB**: Alternating rhymes
- **AABB**: Couplets
- **ABCB**: Second and fourth lines rhyme
- **AAAA**: All lines rhyme

### Meter Considerations
- Keep consistent rhythm
- Match natural speech patterns
- Allow for musical phrasing
- Consider breath marks for singers

### Content Guidelines
- **Character Voice**: Lyrics should sound like the character speaking
- **Plot Advancement**: Move story forward or reveal character
- **Emotional Truth**: Express genuine feelings
- **Specificity**: Use concrete images and details
- **Singability**: Easy to pronounce and remember

### Common Song Functions

#### "I Want" Song
- Express character's deepest desire
- Establish motivation
- Create audience empathy
- Set up character's journey

#### Love Song/Duet
- Express romantic feelings
- Show relationship development
- Create emotional connection
- Often uses metaphor and imagery

#### Comedy Song
- Character-driven humor
- Wordplay and clever rhymes
- Unexpected twists
- Physical comedy opportunities

#### Villain Song
- Reveal antagonist's motivation
- Create menace and threat
- Often grandiose and theatrical
- Show character's worldview

#### Ensemble Number
- Group dynamics
- Community themes
- Multiple perspectives
- Layered vocals
