## Step 1: Generate Your Core Concept (5 minutes)

### Method A: Use the Formula
**Cultural Perspective + Personal Stakes + Universal Theme = Musical Concept**

Pick one from each category:

**Cultural Perspectives:**
- Second-generation immigrant family
- Rural community facing change
- Religious community in transition
- Working-class neighborhood
- Indigenous community
- LGBTQ+ chosen family
- Military family
- Artist community

**Personal Stakes:**
- Protecting family legacy
- Choosing between love and duty
- Overcoming past trauma
- Finding authentic identity
- Saving community space
- Pursuing delayed dreams
- Healing broken relationships
- Standing up to injustice

**Universal Themes:**
- Identity and belonging
- Love vs. obligation
- Tradition vs. progress
- Individual vs. community
- Hope vs. despair
- Truth vs. comfort
- Growth vs. safety
- Justice vs. peace

### Write Your Concept (2 minutes)
**Format:** "When [INCITING INCIDENT], [CULTURAL COMMUNITY] must [GOAL] or else [CONSEQUENCES]."

**Example:** "When their beloved family restaurant faces foreclosure, three generations of a Korean-American family must overcome old grudges and work together to save their American dream before losing everything that connects them to their heritage."

## Step 4: Add Cultural Authenticity (3 minutes)

### Research Checklist
- [ ] **Specific Background**: Not just "Latino" but "Mexican-American from El Paso"
- [ ] **Language Patterns**: How does this community actually speak?
- [ ] **Economic Reality**: What are the financial pressures?
- [ ] **Family Dynamics**: How do generations interact?
- [ ] **Cultural Celebrations**: What traditions matter?
- [ ] **Modern Challenges**: What contemporary issues affect them?

### Quick Authenticity Test
**Can you answer these questions specifically?**
- What does this family eat for Sunday dinner?
- How do they code-switch between languages?
- What's their biggest source of cultural pride?
- What's their biggest cultural conflict?
- How does money affect their cultural expression?

## Step 5: Inject Human Messiness (2 minutes)

### Add Contradictions
For each major character, add something that makes them unpredictable:
- Religious leader who struggles with faith
- Successful person who's financially irresponsible  
- Tough activist who's terrified of confrontation
- Family-oriented person who can't maintain relationships

### Add Secrets
What is each character hiding that complicates their public persona?
- Hidden addiction or mental health issue
- Financial problems masked by success
- Family shame or abandonment
- Educational or professional lies

## üí° Quick Tips

### If You Get Stuck:
- **No clear conflict?** Add a deadline or external pressure
- **Characters feel flat?** Add a secret that complicates their image
- **Story feels generic?** Research a specific cultural community
- **No emotional stakes?** Connect to family, identity, or survival
- **Can't find songs?** Look for moments of high emotion or decision

### Common Beginner Mistakes:
- Making antagonists evil instead of understandable
- Using generic cultural categories instead of specific communities
- Having trauma that only serves plot instead of revealing character
- Creating perfect characters without contradictions
- Focusing on plot instead of emotional journey
