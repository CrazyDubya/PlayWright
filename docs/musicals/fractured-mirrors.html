Synopsis

When Dr. Sarah Chen's experimental memory therapy begins erasing boundaries between her patients' traumas and her own suppressed past, she must navigate a labyrinth of fractured identities to discover which memories are worth saving.

Fractured Mirrors is a psychological journey through memory, trauma, and identity. As Dr. Sarah Chen uses her revolutionary therapy to help patients integrate traumatic memories, she discovers that the boundaries between their experiences and her own suppressed childhood trauma are dissolving. The musical explores how we construct identity from broken pieces and whether some memories should remain buried.

Central Themes

  • Memory and Identity: We are not just our memories, but how we integrate broken pieces
    • \n
  • Healing vs. Avoidance: Can we truly help others while avoiding our own wounds?
    • \n
  • Truth vs. Comfort: Some truths are painful but necessary for authentic healing
    • \n
  • Professional Ethics: The cost of using patients' healing to avoid personal trauma

Principal Characters

Dr. Sarah Chen

Age 35, Mezzo-Soprano

Neuroscientist specializing in trauma-based memory disorders. Uses patients' healing to avoid her own suppressed trauma.

Character Arc: From healer hiding wounds to integrated survivor to authentic therapist.

\n\n

Marcus Torres

Age 28, Tenor

Combat veteran with severe PTSD and memory fragmentation.

Character Arc: From fragmented soldier to integrated person finding peace.

\n\n

Lily Park

Age 22, Soprano

Young woman with dissociative identity disorder from childhood abuse.

Character Arc: From fractured identities to choosing which parts of herself to keep.

\n\n

Dr. Elizabeth Monroe

Age 55, Alto

Sarah's mentor who recognizes Sarah's avoidance patterns.

Character Arc: Wise guide helping Sarah confront her past.
