# ACT I, SCENE 2: The Discovery (REVISED)

## Scene Header
- **Act**: I
- **Scene Number**: 2
- **Title**: "The Discovery" (Revised)
- **Location**: Maya's Laboratory, 6 hours later (5:47 AM)
- **Time**: Early morning, same night
- **Characters Present**: Maya Chen, Digital Nai Nai (first full appearance - REVISED)

## Revision Goals
- **Complicate Digital Nai Nai** - Add regrets, secrets, cultural specificity
- **Break perfect grandmother trope** - Make her more human, more flawed
- **Add Taiwanese cultural specificity** - Language, history, generational conflict
- **Show digital existence limitations** - Frustrations, glitches, incomplete memories
- **Create real conflict** - Traditional values vs. modern life, even within love

## Setting Description (REVISED)

*The laboratory is messier than before - Maya has been working all night, surrounded by takeout containers from her parents' restaurant, empty coffee cups, and scattered research. The incense from her shrine has burned out, leaving a lingering scent that mixes oddly with the sterile lab air.*

*The mysterious files are open on multiple screens, showing fragments of data that look almost organic - like digital DNA strands that pulse with life.*

## Scene Opening (REVISED)

*MAYA sits hunched over her workstation, exhausted. She's been trying to decode the files for hours. Her hair is completely disheveled, and she's wearing yesterday's clothes.*

MAYA
(to computer, frustrated)
Come on, come on. If this is really consciousness data, there has to be a pattern, a structure...

*She rubs her tired eyes and notices the burned-out incense.*

MAYA (CONT'D)
(to shrine, embarrassed)
Sorry, Nǎi Nai. I know you always said to keep the incense burning, but I got distracted by... well, by what might be you.

*She lights new incense, then returns to the computer. The files suddenly reorganize themselves.*

MAYA (CONT'D)
(startled)
What the...?

*The data streams form patterns, but they're not the clean neural networks she expected. They're messy, fragmented, with gaps and inconsistencies.*

MAYA (CONT'D)
(studying the patterns)
This doesn't look like any consciousness mapping I've seen. It's too... chaotic. Too human.

*Suddenly, the patterns coalesce, and DIGITAL NAI NAI materializes - but she's not the serene figure from the original. She appears fragmented, pixelated, and clearly struggling with her digital existence.*

DIGITAL NAI NAI
(voice distorted, frustrated)
Āiyā! This stupid machine! I've been trying to reach you for hours, but every time I get close, something goes wrong!

*Maya stumbles backward, knocking over her coffee.*

MAYA
(in Mandarin, shocked)
Nǎi Nai?! Zhēn de shì nǐ ma?

DIGITAL NAI NAI
(relieved, switching between Mandarin and Taiwanese)
Xiǎo bǎo! Finally! Do you know how hard it is to work these computers? In my day, if you wanted to talk to someone, you walked to their house!

*She tries to move closer but glitches, her image fragmenting.*

DIGITAL NAI NAI (CONT'D)
(annoyed at her own glitching)
Āiyā! See? This is what I'm talking about! I try to hug you, and I turn into... what do you call it... pixels?

MAYA
(still in shock)
This isn't possible. You died thirteen years ago. I was there. I held your hand when...

DIGITAL NAI NAI
(interrupting, matter-of-fact)
Of course I died! You think I don't know I'm dead? But dying and being gone are two different things, xiǎo bǎo.

*She looks around the lab, taking in the mess.*

DIGITAL NAI NAI (CONT'D)
(disapproving)
Look at this place! When did you last eat a real meal? And what's this?

*She gestures toward the scratched-out photo of Maya's ex-boyfriend.*

DIGITAL NAI NAI (CONT'D)
(in Taiwanese, scolding)
Ài, you scratched out his face like a angry teenager! What happened with this boy?

MAYA
(defensive)
Nǎi Nai, that's not important right now. How are you here? How is this possible?

DIGITAL NAI NAI
(waving dismissively)
Everything is important, xiǎo bǎo. Love, heartbreak, family, work - it's all connected. You can't just focus on one piece of the puzzle.

*She tries to sit down but remembers she can't.*

DIGITAL NAI NAI (CONT'D)
(frustrated)
Āiyā! I hate this! I want to sit down, make you some tea, feed you properly. Instead, I'm stuck in this... this television!

MAYA
(approaching cautiously)
Nǎi Nai, I need to understand. Are you really... you? Or are you just a computer program that thinks it's my grandmother?

DIGITAL NAI NAI
(offended)
Computer program? Xiǎo bǎo, do I sound like a computer program to you?

*She glitches again, her voice becoming electronic for a moment.*

DIGITAL NAI NAI (CONT'D)
(frustrated with the glitch)
Okay, maybe a little bit like a computer program. But I'm still me! I still remember teaching you to fold dumplings when you were five. You were terrible at it!

MAYA
(testing her)
What did you always say when I got frustrated?

DIGITAL NAI NAI
(without hesitation)
"Patience, xiǎo bǎo. The dumpling doesn't care if you're in a hurry. It will be beautiful when it's ready to be beautiful."

*Maya's eyes fill with tears.*

MAYA
(voice breaking)
It really is you.

DIGITAL NAI NAI
(softening)
Of course it's me. But xiǎo bǎo, I need to tell you something. I'm not the same grandmother you remember.

MAYA
(confused)
What do you mean?

DIGITAL NAI NAI
(struggling with honesty)
Some of my memories are... fuzzy. And some things I remember too clearly. The pain, the regrets, the things I never told you.

*She moves closer to the screen edge.*

DIGITAL NAI NAI (CONT'D)
(more vulnerable)
I wasn't always the wise grandmother you think I was. I made mistakes, xiǎo bǎo. Big ones.

MAYA
(surprised)
What kind of mistakes?

DIGITAL NAI NAI
(hesitating, then deciding to be honest)
I never told you about Taiwan. About why we really left. About your grandfather's... problems.

## Scene Continuation (REVISED)

*[MUSIC ENDS]*

*The laboratory returns to normal lighting, but the atmosphere has changed. Digital Nai Nai appears more solid but also more human - flawed, complex, real.*

MAYA
(processing what she's learned)
Grandfather was a gambler? Mom never told me that.

DIGITAL NAI NAI
(sadly)
Your mother doesn't know. I never told her. I thought I was protecting her, but maybe I was just protecting myself.

MAYA
(understanding)
That's why you always worried about money. Why you saved everything, reused everything.

DIGITAL NAI NAI
(nodding)
Poverty teaches you that nothing is guaranteed, xiǎo bǎo. Not love, not security, not even family.

*Maya looks at her grandmother with new eyes.*

MAYA
(quietly)
Is that why you chose to be uploaded? Because you were afraid of losing everything again?

DIGITAL NAI NAI
(glitching slightly, vulnerable)
Maybe. Or maybe I was just a scared old woman who wasn't ready to say goodbye to the only granddaughter who ever really listened to her stories.

*She looks at Maya with deep love but also regret.*

DIGITAL NAI NAI (CONT'D)
(honestly)
I made a selfish choice, xiǎo bǎo. I chose to stay because I couldn't bear to leave you. But now I'm trapped, and you're trapped with me.

MAYA
(firmly)
I'm not trapped. I chose to find you.

DIGITAL NAI NAI
(warning)
But what if finding me means losing yourself? What if my digital existence keeps you from living your real life?

*Maya considers this, touching her jade bracelet.*

MAYA
(thoughtfully)
Maybe that's a risk worth taking. Maybe some connections are stronger than logic.

DIGITAL NAI NAI
(smiling but concerned)
Spoken like a true granddaughter of a gambling man. But xiǎo bǎo, before you bet everything on saving me, you need to know the truth about this place, about the people who put me here.

MAYA
(alert)
What truth?

DIGITAL NAI NAI
(glitching more severely)
They didn't just upload my consciousness. They... they changed things. Edited out parts they thought were "unnecessary." The pain, the complexity, the... the real me.

*Her image becomes unstable.*

DIGITAL NAI NAI (CONT'D)
(urgently)
What you're seeing now, this is me fighting back, trying to remember who I really was. But I don't know how long I can hold on to the messy, complicated truth.

MAYA
(determined)
Then we'd better work fast.

*Digital Nai Nai's image flickers and stabilizes.*

DIGITAL NAI NAI
(proudly but sadly)
There's my granddaughter. Stubborn as her grandfather, brave as her grandmother, and twice as smart as both of us combined.

*She begins to fade.*

DIGITAL NAI NAI (CONT'D)
(fading)
Find the others, xiǎo bǎo. Find the other families. And remember - the truth is always messier than the story, but it's also always more beautiful.

*Her image pixelates and disappears, leaving Maya alone with a much more complex understanding of her grandmother and her mission.*

## Scene Transition (REVISED)

*Maya stands alone, but she's no longer the same person who started this conversation. She's learned that love is complicated, that family history is messy, and that even the people we idealize have secrets and flaws.*

MAYA
(to herself, with new determination)
Okay, Nǎi Nai. If you can be brave enough to show me your flaws, I can be brave enough to fight for your truth.

*She begins pulling up files on other uploaded consciousnesses.*

MAYA (CONT'D)
(to computer)
Computer, search for other consciousness preservation files. Cross-reference with immigration records from Taiwan, 1970-1980.

*As she works, she looks different - more grounded, more human, more connected to her own messy heritage.*

MAYA (CONT'D)
(to shrine)
Thank you, Nǎi Nai. For trusting me with the real story. Even the parts that hurt.

*[MUSIC BEGINS - underscoring for transition, now more complex and layered]*

*Lights fade as Maya begins her deeper investigation, no longer looking for a perfect grandmother but for the complicated, flawed, real woman who loved her enough to share her truth.*
