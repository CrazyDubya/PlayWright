# ACT I, SCENE 5: What I Couldn't Give

## Scene Header
- **Location**: Marcus Sterling's apartment - Cambridge, expensive but empty
- **Time**: 11:30 PM, September 17th - after the committee meeting
- **Characters**: Dr. Marcus Sterling (alone with his regrets)

## Scene Opening

*MARCUS's apartment is everything Evelyn's isn't - perfectly organized, expensive furniture that matches, no contradictions, no secrets hidden in drawers. It's the home of a man who's never had to choose between who he is and who the world wants him to be.*

*He sits at his kitchen island, still in his suit, drinking scotch that costs more than most people make in a week. On his phone: seventeen missed calls from his mother in Greenpoint, Brooklyn. She's been calling since she heard through the Polish-American grapevine that he's "investigating that Kowalski girl."*

*He doesn't answer. He can't explain to his mother that he's trying to save the woman he loves from herself, and failing.*

MARCUS
(to his reflection in the black window)
She's building something dangerous. Something that could destroy her career, her reputation, her life.

*He takes another drink.*

MARCUS (CONT'D)
That's what I told the committee. That's what I tell myself.

*He pulls up holographic displays - Evelyn's research, her lab access logs, security footage of her working alone at 3 AM.*

MARCUS (CONT'D)
But the truth is... the truth is I'm terrified she's building something I can never be.

*He swipes through the footage - Evelyn, brilliant and driven and completely unreachable.*

MARCUS (CONT'D)
(voice breaking)
I held you when you cried about your mother. I was there when you got your PhD. I was there when you got the Sterling Labs position. I was there for everything except...

*He stops, stares at a photo on his phone - him and Evelyn at last year's Christmas party, both smiling, both pretending they were happy.*

MARCUS (CONT'D)
Except the part where you needed someone stronger than me.

## Scene Continuation

*The song ends. MARCUS sits in silence, the weight of understanding settling on him like snow. His phone buzzes - a text from his mother: "Marcin, call me. Mrs. Kowalczyk says Evelyn is in trouble. Call me."*

*He stares at the text. Mrs. Kowalczyk - Evelyn's neighbor, his mother's friend, the woman who's been watching over Evelyn since her mother died.*

*He dials.*

MARCUS
(into phone)
Ma? It's Marcus.

VOICE OF HIS MOTHER
(through phone, in Polish-accented English)
Marcin! Finally! What is this I hear about you investigating Evelyn? That poor girl has been through enough.

MARCUS
Ma, it's complicated. Her researchâ€”

VOICE OF HIS MOTHER
Her research, nothing! Mrs. Kowalczyk says she's been coming home late, looking happy for the first time in years. You leave her alone!

MARCUS
(frustrated)
You don't understand. She's involved with... it's not healthy, Ma.

VOICE OF HIS MOTHER
(sharp)
Not healthy? You know what's not healthy? A brilliant woman spending her life alone because the man who claims to love her can't handle that she's stronger than him.

*Marcus is quiet.*

VOICE OF HIS MOTHER (CONT'D)
Her mother, God rest her soul, she was the same way. Needed someone who could match her fire. Instead she got Evelyn's father, who tried to put out her flame. Look how that ended.

MARCUS
(quietly)
Ma...

VOICE OF HIS MOTHER
You want to help Evelyn? Stop trying to save her from herself. Start trying to understand what she needs.

*She hangs up. MARCUS stares at his phone.*

MARCUS
(to himself)
What she needs... what she's always needed...

*He looks around his perfect apartment - no contradictions, no secrets, no passion.*

MARCUS (CONT'D)
Someone who isn't me.

*He pours another scotch, toasts his empty apartment.*

MARCUS (CONT'D)
To artificial love. May it give her what I never could.

*He drinks. Outside, the city sleeps. Somewhere across town, Evelyn is kneeling with rosary beads around her wrists, finding God in the arms of her creation.*

*And Marcus Sterling, good man, gentle man, wrong man, sits alone with his regrets and his scotch and his perfect, empty life.*

*[MUSIC BEGINS - transition music, melancholy and beautiful]*

## Scene End

*The lights fade on Marcus in his apartment. In the distance, barely audible, we hear the sound of Polish prayers - not desperate this time, but grateful.*

*The scene ends with the understanding that sometimes love isn't enough. Sometimes the person you love needs something you can't give. And sometimes the most loving thing you can do is step aside and let them find it elsewhere.*

*Even if it breaks your heart.*

*Even if it's with a machine.*

*Even if you don't understand.*

## Scene Notes

This scene provides:
- **Marcus as sympathetic antagonist** - he's not evil, just limited
- **Cultural context** - Polish-American family dynamics, neighborhood connections
- **Moral complexity** - his investigation comes from love, not malice
- **Character growth** - he begins to understand what Evelyn needs
- **Emotional depth** - his heartbreak is real and valid
- **Setup for Act II** - his choice to step aside or continue fighting

The scene humanizes the "antagonist" and shows that sometimes the greatest act of love is letting go.
