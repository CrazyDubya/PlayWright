# PICKET FENCE PRISON: A Suburban Power Dynamic Musical

## TRANSCENDENT CREATION PROTOCOL - MATURE ADULT MUSICAL

**Target Audience**: Adults 25+ (Mature themes, explicit power dynamics, sexual content)
**Genre**: Suburban Psychological Musical with BDSM/Power Exchange Elements
**Setting**: Present-day affluent suburban cul-de-sac where digital screens have drained life but biology keeps ticking

---

## ‚ö° **CORE CONCEPT**

**Logline**: When empty-nest housewife CLAIRE MORRISON discovers that a mysterious neighbor has been orchestrating elaborate power games throughout their pristine cul-de-sac, she must choose between the safety of her picket fence prison and the intoxicating danger of surrendering control to desires she never knew she had.

**Central Theme**: In a world where #MeToo has sanitized desire and digital screens have replaced human connection, what happens when biology demands what society has forbidden? Can authentic power exchange heal the numbness of suburban perfection, or does it only create new forms of beautiful imprisonment?

## üé® **VISUAL & STAGING CONCEPTS**

### **Set Design**
- Multi-level set showing cross-sections of suburban homes
- Pristine front yards that rotate to reveal messy interiors
- Hidden spaces (basements, attics) where real life happens
- Video screens throughout showing the digital world that distracts from reality

### **Costume Design**
- Suburban perfection gradually peeling away
- Hidden elements (lingerie under cardigans, toys concealed in purses)
- Color palette shifts from pastels to deeper, richer tones as characters awaken
- David's costumes suggest control without being obvious

### **Choreography**
- Domestic movements transformed into dance
- Power dynamics expressed through levels and positioning
- "Marionette" as centerpiece - actual suspension work with trained performers
- Ensemble pieces showing the conformity and hidden rebellion of suburban life

## üí´ **THE TRANSCENDENT PROMISE**

This isn't just another suburban satire or another "women behaving badly" story. This is a genuine exploration of what happens when we stop performing our lives and start living them. It asks the dangerous question: What if the things we're not supposed to want are exactly what we need?

In a world where desire has been sanitized and power dynamics have been simplified, PICKET FENCE PRISON dares to suggest that the path to authentic connection might require walking through some very uncomfortable territory.
