## SYSTEM 8: BREAKING ENGINE - MEDIOCRITY SCAN

### TARGET 1: DAVID STERLING - "Puppet Master"
**PROBLEM**: Too purely manipulative. Convenient villain.
**INJECTION**: His dead wife wasn't just "submissive" - she was his dominant for 15 years. He only learned to top after she died, trying to become her. He's not a natural master - he's cosplaying his grief. The control is PERFORMANCE.

### TARGET 2: JESSICA'S "PREMIUM CONTENT"
**PROBLEM**: Too convenient that she monetizes her journey.
**INJECTION**: The money goes to her birth mother's medical bills in Korea. Jessica's never met her. The performance isn't greed - it's the only connection she can have to origin. She degrades herself to save a woman she'll never know.

### TARGET 3: MICHAEL'S EMOTIONAL ABSENCE
**PROBLEM**: He's a convenient villain (neglectful husband).
**INJECTION**: Michael had an affair with a man 8 years ago. He ended it. He's been emotionally dead since because he killed the part of himself that felt. His "Customer #247" isn't voyeurism - it's trying to feel ANYTHING through someone else's screen.

### TARGET 4: THE HAPPY ENDING ADJACENT
**PROBLEM**: Current ending lets Claire "choose complexity." Too clean.
**INJECTION**: Claire's daughter visits. Finds out everything. Is disgusted. Claire must face that her awakening has a cost she can't control. Freedom isn't free - someone else pays.

### TARGET 5: MISSING BODY
**PROBLEM**: Lots of BDSM discussed, little shown in consequence.
**INJECTION**: Add a scene where Claire can't sit down at a neighborhood meeting. Her body carries evidence. The physical can't be hidden from the social. The flesh betrays what the face conceals.

## SYSTEM 10: RITUAL FRAMEWORK - SACRED ARCHITECTURE

### Transformation Objective:
The audience will leave UNABLE to see suburban houses the same way. Every cul-de-sac will whisper. Every perfect lawn will be suspect. Every "fine, thanks" will be a lie.

### The Threshold (Pre-Show):
- Audience enters through a "model home" staging area
- Realtors (actors) offer welcome packets
- Glade PlugIn scent pumped through space
- Screens show perfect family photos (all slightly wrong)
- **The crossing**: They sign a "non-disclosure agreement" about what they'll witness
- They are now COMPLICIT. Witnesses. Voyeurs. Neighbors.

### The Ordeal (The Performance):
- Audience seated in "neighborhood watch" sections
- Ring Doorbell footage plays between scenes (of THEM)
- During "Supermarket Seduction" - audience plants revealed among them
- Intermission: served wine in Claire's kitchen set
- They become the neighborhood that watches, judges, envies

### The Transformation (The Climax):
- "Breaking Point" - house walls literally fall outward
- All secrets visible. All spaces exposed. No hiding.
- Audience can see each other across fallen walls
- They are each other's neighbors now
- A mirror: What are YOU hiding behind your fence?

### The Return (The Ending):
- No curtain call until audience exits through broken model home
- "Welcome packets" now contain blank "What We Saw" cards
- They leave carrying the secret they brought
- The ritual doesn't end - they take it home

### Audience Role Assignment:
- **Section A - The Judgers**: Seated highest, given "observation" notes
- **Section B - The Curious**: Eye-level, closest to action
- **Section C - The Complicit**: Lowest, hidden - see what others miss, most implicated
