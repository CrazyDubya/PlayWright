# CLAIRE MORRISON - Lead Female Protagonist

## Basic Information
- **Age**: 42
- **Occupation**: Former marketing executive, now "professional volunteer" and empty-nester
- **Cultural Background**: Third-generation Irish-Catholic from Boston, transplanted to suburban Atlanta
- **Physical Description**: Still beautiful but trying too hard - perfectly styled blonde hair, designer athleisure, manicured nails that tap nervously
- **Voice Type**: Mezzo-soprano with rich lower register for intimate moments

## Character Arc

### **Want vs. Need**
- **Want**: To feel alive, desired, and significant again
- **Need**: To reclaim her authentic self beneath years of suburban performance and stop seeking validation through perfection

### **Fatal Flaw**
Perfectionism that masks deep shame about her desires. She's spent 25 years being the "good wife" to prove she's not like her mother, but this has created a prison of her own making.

### **Character Journey**
- **Act I**: Awakening to desires she's suppressed for decades
- **Act II**: Navigating the dangerous territory between safety and authenticity
- **Resolution**: Choosing to live with the complexity of her desires rather than hiding from them

## Background & Trauma

### **Family Secret**
When Claire was 16, she discovered her mother Margaret having an affair with their parish priest. The scandal destroyed the family - her father left, her mother had a nervous breakdown, and Claire had to raise her younger siblings. She's spent her adult life being the perfect wife to prove she's nothing like her mother.

### **Economic Pressure**
Upper-middle class comfort that feels like a golden cage. Michael's income allows her to volunteer and maintain appearances, but she has no independent financial power. The house, cars, and lifestyle are all in his name.

### **Cultural Trauma**
Irish-Catholic guilt around sexuality and pleasure. Raised to believe that "good women" don't have desires, that sex is for procreation, that wanting pleasure makes you selfish and dangerous.

## Relationships

### **With Michael (Husband)**
- 20 years of marriage that started passionate but became routine
- He provides security but no emotional intimacy
- She performs the role of "perfect wife" while feeling invisible
- Their sex life died years ago but neither acknowledges it

### **With David (Dom)**
- Represents everything forbidden and dangerous
- He sees through her performance to her authentic desires
- Their dynamic awakens parts of herself she thought were dead
- The relationship forces her to confront her shame and fear

### **With Jessica (Rival/Friend)**
- Initially sees Jessica as competition for David's attention
- Gradually realizes they're both trapped in the same game
- Their rivalry becomes understanding as they recognize shared trauma
- Potential for genuine friendship if they can move past competition

### **With Her Children**
- Twin daughters, now 19 and away at college
- Claire defined herself as "mother" for 20 years
- Empty nest syndrome triggered her crisis of identity
- Fears her daughters will judge her if they discover her desires

## Contradictions & Complexity

### **Public vs. Private**
- **Public**: Champions women's empowerment, volunteers for feminist causes
- **Private**: Craves surrender and being controlled by a strong man
- **Conflict**: How can she reconcile her political beliefs with her sexual desires?

### **Control vs. Surrender**
- **Surface**: Appears to have her life perfectly controlled
- **Reality**: Feels completely out of control and desperate for someone else to take charge
- **Growth**: Learning that choosing to surrender is actually a form of control

### **Mother vs. Woman**
- **Past Identity**: Defined herself entirely through motherhood
- **Current Crisis**: Who is she when she's not needed as a mother?
- **Future**: Integrating her roles as mother, wife, and sexual being

## Key Scenes & Songs

### **"Picket Fence Prison" (Opening)**
Claire's morning routine reveals the emptiness beneath suburban perfection. Every action is performed for an invisible audience - the neighbors, social media, her own internalized judge.

*Sample lyrics:*
```

Volunteer meeting, can't be late

But these picket fences feel like bars
```

### **"No Panties Tomorrow" (with David)**
The night that changes everything. A neighborhood party, too much wine, a flirtatious dance that leads to David's first command. The song builds from playful banter to serious power exchange.

*Key moment:*
```
DAVID: (low, commanding)
No panties tomorrow when you go to the store
Let every step remind you of what you're living for

CLAIRE: (breathless, shocked)
I can't... I'm not... that's not who I am

DAVID: (gentle but firm)
That's exactly who you are, Claire
You just forgot how to let her breathe
```

### **"Marionette" (Act II Ballet/Bondage Piece)**
Claire's moment of complete surrender. Choreographed with actual suspension work, this is her most vulnerable moment - and her most powerful. She realizes that choosing to give up control is the ultimate act of self-determination.

## Character Voice & Dialogue

### **Speech Patterns**
- Starts sentences then stops herself ("I just... never mind")
- Uses qualifiers and apologies ("I'm sorry, but maybe...")
- Gradually becomes more direct as she finds her voice
- Irish expressions emerge when she's emotional ("Jesus, Mary, and Joseph")

### **Internal Monologue**
- Constant self-criticism and second-guessing
- Battles between what she wants and what she thinks she should want
- Memories of her mother's shame bleeding into her own experience
- Growing awareness of her own power and agency

## Costume & Visual Evolution

### **Act I Beginning**
- Perfectly coordinated athleisure in neutral tones
- Hair always styled, makeup always perfect
- Jewelry that's expensive but conservative
- Posture that's controlled and careful

### **Act I Development**
- Colors become richer, more saturated
- Clothing fits her body better, shows more skin
- Hair becomes looser, more natural
- Movements become more fluid and confident

### **Act II**
- Clothing that suggests hidden elements (lace under cardigans)
- Colors that reflect her emotional state
- For "Marionette" - costume designed for suspension work
- Final scene - still suburban but authentically herself
